Nebraska Homestead Exemption Details

Nebraska's homestead exemption protects up to $60,000 of equity in your primary residence. This covers houses, condos, and mobile homes you occupy as your primary dwelling. Key rules:

  • The property must be your primary residence
  • The exemption protects equity (value minus mortgage and liens)
  • Head of household does not need to be married
  • Rural homesteads are limited to 160 acres

Important Notes for Nebraska Filers

  • No federal exemptions: Nebraska has opted out. You must use state exemptions.
  • No wildcard exemption: Nebraska does not offer a general-purpose wildcard. Every asset must fit within a specific exemption category.
  • Low vehicle exemption: At $2,400, Nebraska's vehicle exemption is among the lowest. If your car equity exceeds this, consider Chapter 13 to keep the vehicle.
  • Strong retirement protection: All ERISA-qualified plans and IRAs are fully exempt - no dollar cap.
  • Domicile rule: You must have been domiciled in Nebraska for 730 days (2 years) before filing. Otherwise, use your prior state's exemptions.
